About a week and half ago, Shelley gave birth to our beautiful daughter, Phoenix Courville. It was the happiest, most emotional and intense moment of my life.

As for most new parents, the time since then has been a little chaotic. Sometimes we feel amazing, other times, we're plagued with self-doubt.

However, I've noticed a few things that have changed with how I approach work.

The first is that systems really pay back dividends. We've been handling the transition well, in no small part because we have systems in place to deal with meal prep and getting things done around the house.

In the past, sometimes it felt like we were just spinning our wheels with all these systems, but when shit hits the fan, it's great to have something to fall back on. We're still eating healthy and moving our projects forward, despite having this little one who requires constant attention.

The second is that having a kid really puts things in perspective. Unimportant tasks are easier to weed out and it's easier to take action on the things that **are** important. I feel emboldened to tackle challenges that seemed too daunting before. Now that our bundle is in production, it's easier to not screw around with unimportant things.

Up until now, I felt like all this freelancing and blogging was a big experiment that I would ride until it stopped working.

Now though, things feel completely different. I want more structure. I want more systems in place to generate leads, to meet new people, to reach new audiences. I want to multiply my impact and help as many people as possible.

I'm more excited than ever to see what the future has in store.

Let's make that happen 💪🏻
